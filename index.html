📖 Our History

Cricket was brought to India a long time ago by British sailors. We played our very first Test match in the year 1932 at Lord's in England. Our first captain was C. K. Nayudu. Over the years, cricket became the most popular sport in our country. It is almost like a religion here! 🏟️

You can read the full history on Wikipedia's Team India page.

🥇 World Cups & Trophies Won

Here is a simple timeline of when we won the biggest cups:

  • 📅 1983: Won our first ODI World Cup! (Captain: Kapil Dev) 🏆
  • 📅 2007: Won the first ever T20 World Cup! (Captain: MS Dhoni) 🏆
  • 📅 2011: Won the second ODI World Cup at home! (Captain: MS Dhoni) 🏆
  • 📅 2013: Won the Champions Trophy in England! (Captain: MS Dhoni) 🏅
  • 📅 2023: Reached the finals of the ODI World Cup after an incredible 10-match winning streak! (Captain: Rohit Sharma) 🥈
  • 📅 2024: Won the ICC Men's T20 World Cup in Barbados, defeating South Africa by 7 runs in a thrilling final! (Captain: Rohit Sharma) 🏆
  • 📅 2026: Won the ICC Men's T20 World Cup at home, defeating New Zealand by 96 runs in the final at Ahmedabad! (Captain: Suryakumar Yadav) 🏆

🏟️ Famous Stadiums

India has some of the biggest and loudest cricket stadiums in the world!

  • Narendra Modi Stadium (Ahmedabad): The largest cricket stadium in the world! It can hold over 130,000 people.
  • Eden Gardens (Kolkata): Known as the "Mecca of Indian cricket".
  • Wankhede Stadium (Mumbai): Where India won the 2011 World Cup final! 🎉
